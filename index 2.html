Week 1 — Foundation
Establish structure. Strength is heavy and compound-focused. Circuits are controlled and moderate. Station day is technique-first — no time pressure. Runs stay aerobic (Z2) with short bursts of race pace.

// Program Notes
Weekly Structure
Mon = Run. Tue = Strength + Circuit. Wed = Rest. Thu = Strength + Circuit. Fri = Run. Sat = HYROX Stations. Sun = Rest. W4 exception: Thu = last strength session, Fri = Rest, Sat = Race.
Sled Prep
Sled push is 80% leg drive — back squat, trap bar deadlift and Bulgarian split squats build this directly. Sled pull is horizontal pulling — bent-over row and weighted pull-ups transfer directly. The RDL added to Thu builds the hip hinge lockout at the end of each sled stride. Practice both push and pull on every Sat station day.
Strength Philosophy
Heavy compound lifts (squat, deadlift, pull) build the structural base for sled, carry, and lunges. Never skip the strength block just to get more circuit reps in — you need both.
Circuit Philosophy
Circuits train you to work at a station after arriving gassed from a run. Wall balls and KB lunges in circuits directly reinforce station-specific endurance. The run-embedded circuit on Tue is the most race-specific training you'll do.
Mixed HYROX Weights
Sled Push: +102/+52 kg. Sled Pull: +103/+53 kg. Farmers: 2×24/2×16 kg. Sandbag: 20/10 kg. Wall Balls: 6/4 kg. KB Lunges: 2×24/2×16 kg.
Solo Training Tips
Use a GPS watch or phone to auto-split each km during runs. For the simulation, set a timer per station. On race day in Mixed HYROX, you and your partner each complete your own running segments — your individual station performance is where you control the clock.
Nutrition
2g protein/kg BW daily. Carb-load Fri–Sat before race. Race morning: carb meal 2–3 hrs prior. Gel every 20–25 min during race, sip water at every station entry.
